About Me

Hi, I'm Naveed Arshad, a second year Electronics & Instrumentation Engineering student with a strong interest in understanding systems from first principles. I'm deeply curious about how things work at a fundamental level, and I prefer breaking concepts down to their core mechanics before building them back up into complete systems.
I work on building and simulating systems that range from digital hardware concepts to computational models that demonstrate how complex behavior can emerge from simple underlying rules. Whether it's exploring logic at the hardware level or modeling interactions in a simulated environment, I focus on understanding the structure, constraints, and dynamics that drive system behavior.
Most of my learning happens through hands-on experimentation. I write simulations, construct models, and test ideas practically rather than relying only on theory. For me, building is a way of thinking, it helps me uncover how systems function beneath the surface and how small rule sets can scale into intricate, large-scale behavior.
At present, I'm focused on strengthening my understanding of integrated circuit design across both analog and digital domains, with an emphasis on core principles and underlying design concepts. I try to understand the reasoning behind design decisions, the trade-offs involved, and the constraints that shape circuit behavior, rather than approaching it only from a tools or application perspective.
Alongside this, I'm actively learning and implementing machine learning techniques through personal, experiment-driven projects. My aim is to understand how learning algorithms actually work , how models update, how optimization functions, and how patterns are formed, instead of just using existing frameworks. I prefer building small implementations to observe how changes in structure or parameters affect results.
At the same time, I'm developing my front-end web development skills as a separate domain, focusing on clean, responsive, and well-structured interfaces that clearly present systems and simulations.

Beyond academics, I actively document my learning journey and share insights online. I regularly post on LinkedIn about my projects, experiences, and the lessons I learn along the way. In parallel, I work on a variety of projects, which are available on my GitHub. This practice helps me deepen my understanding while also connecting with a wider community of learners and professionals.

Feel free to explore my work and connect with me on LinkedIn and GitHub to follow my journey and exchange ideas.
